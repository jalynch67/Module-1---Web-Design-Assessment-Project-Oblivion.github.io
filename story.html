The Story of Oblivion

Step into a world on the brink of destruction.

Main Story Journey

Follow the rise of a hero and the fall of an empire.

You begin as a prisoner, unexpectedly visited by Emperor Uriel Septim. Moments later, assassins strike, and you escape through hidden tunnels beneath the Imperial City.

With the Emperor dead, gates to Oblivion begin opening across the land, unleashing Daedric forces upon Tamriel.

You must find the last heir, Martin Septim, and help him reclaim the throne to stop the invasion.

In a final confrontation, Martin sacrifices himself to banish Mehrunes Dagon and save Tamriel forever.

Oblivion Gates

Oblivion Gates are portals that tear open across Tamriel following the death of Emperor Uriel Septim VII. Each gate leads into a Daedric realm ruled by Mehrunes Dagon, filled with lava fields, hostile Dremora, and twisted towers of black stone.

Inside each realm, the central objective is the Sigil Tower, where a Sigil Stone powers the gate. By fighting through Daedra and navigating dangerous terrain, you reach the top and remove the Sigil Stone, collapsing the gate and sealing the connection to Oblivion.

Every closed gate weakens the Daedric invasion and provides powerful enchanted Sigil Stones that can be used to enhance weapons and armor.

Battle of Kvatch

The Battle of Kvatch marks the first major Daedric invasion of a city in Cyrodiil. The city is overrun after an Oblivion Gate opens nearby, allowing Daedra to pour into the streets.

When you arrive, Kvatch is already in ruins, with survivors regrouping at the camp led by Savlian Matius. Your mission becomes critical: enter the burning Oblivion Gate outside the city, rescue survivors trapped inside, and close the portal from within.

Once the gate is closed, you help lead the remaining guards in a counterattack to reclaim Kvatch from the Daedric forces.

The Final Sacrifice

At the end of the crisis, Martin Septim travels to the Imperial City carrying the Amulet of Kings, the key to restoring the Dragonfires and stopping Mehrunes Dagon’s full manifestation in Nirn.

As the final Oblivion Gate opens within the Imperial City, Martin makes the ultimate decision. He breaks the Amulet of Kings, calling upon the power of Akatosh, the Dragon God of Time.

Transformed into a massive divine avatar of Akatosh, Martin battles Mehrunes Dagon directly. The Daedric Prince is banished back to Oblivion, the Dragonfires are restored, and the invasion is ended— but Martin’s sacrifice marks the end of the Septim bloodline.
